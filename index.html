Electric Car Market

Objective

Analyze electric vehicle (EV) performance based on acceleration, top speed, range, efficiency, and charging speed. Compare different EV brands and models in terms of powertrain, body style, and price. Identify trends in EV efficiency, charging capabilities, and affordability. Help consumers and industry professionals make informed decisions about EV purchase and market positioning.

Conclusion

Tesla and premium brands offer better acceleration, range, and fast charging speeds but at a higher price. Efficiency (Wh/Km) varies across models, impacting running costs and long-term savings. Fast charging capability is a key feature for long-distance usability, with significant differences between brands. Hatchbacks and compact EVs are more affordable but typically have lower range and slower acceleration.

Customer Segmentation

Objective

Analyze the impact of age, profession, marital status, education, dependents, and salary on financial decisions. Identify trends in personal loan approvals based on demographics and income levels. Understand the relationship between total salary and purchase price of assets. Assist financial institutions in loan risk assessment and customer segmentation. Provide insights into spending behavior based on profession and marital status.

Conclusion

Higher salary individuals tend to afford higher-priced assets, but not all take personal loans. Business professionals and salaried employees differ in spending and borrowing habits, with salaried individuals often having higher income stability. Marital status and number of dependents influence financial decisions, affecting loan approvals and asset purchases.
